The Economist — “Big tech is sacrificing its cashflows to prop up the AI boom / Kamikaze capex”

  • Date: 2026-05-13
  • URL: archived locally at Kamikaze capex.md
  • What it argues: The five US cloud giants (Amazon, Alphabet, Meta, Microsoft, Oracle) will spend $800bn on AI infra in 2026 (~40% of revenue), surpassing telecom dotcom capex in scale. Profits keep rising on accounting basis but free cash flow is collapsing; buybacks have stopped. The Big Five have raised $260bn in bonds since start-2025 (~25% of all listed non-financial US issuance). Off-balance-sheet: $820bn of future data-centre lease payments not yet commenced (up from $270bn a year ago) plus $680bn of other future obligations. Meta’s Louisiana SPV bond was largest single corporate bond in history. Oracle CFO has talked of “uncoupling” capex from cashflow via similar engineering. Capex projections for 2026 are 2× what analysts forecast a year ago. Frames the dynamic as “central planning” by hyperscalers across the AI supply chain — sacrificing their own returns to keep the value chain solvent, while Broadcom/Micron/Nvidia/Sandisk capture a quarter of S&P 500 forecast profit growth this year.
  • Verify: $800bn 2026 capex aggregate; $260bn bond issuance figure; $820bn lease commitments figure; “2trn future revenue agreements” up from $730bn at Amazon/Google/Microsoft/Oracle.
